Ethics Abena Koomson-Davis · akoomson-davis@ecfs.org · official instructions ↗

This year's theme is empathy - how it helps build a healthy, safe, brave community.

"To care for anyone else enough to make their problems one's own, is ever the beginning of one's real ethical development." - Felix Adler, ECFS founder

Write 1-2 pages about a time someone empathized with you in a meaningful way - or a time you empathized with someone else - and what it changed.

History Keira Lapsley · klapsley@ecfs.org · official instructions ↗

Choose one coming-of-age book set outside the US. Some cover serious, difficult material - worth reading with your family.

Show the 7 books - choose one
Born a Crime - Trevor Noah South Africa
Growing up mixed-race under apartheid, when his existence was illegal.
Now Is the Time for Running - Michael Williams Zimbabwe
Deo and his brother flee their village after soldiers arrive, becoming refugees.
Revolution Is Not a Dinner Party - Ying Chang Compestine China
A doctor's daughter navigates the Cultural Revolution as her family is targeted.
Behind the Mountains - Edwidge Danticat Haiti / NYC
Celiane survives political violence in Haiti, then adjusts to a new life in Brooklyn.
Farewell to Manzanar - Jeanne Wakatsuki Houston & James D. Houston US
A Japanese-American family's true story of internment during WWII.
The Long Season of Rain - Helen S. Kim Korea
Four sisters navigate their parents' friction and an orphaned boy their family takes in.
Chinese Cinderella - Adeline Yen Mah China
A memoir of an unwanted daughter's painful childhood and eventual triumph.

After reading, write two paragraphs on how the main character was shaped by (1) their identity - ability, age, ethnicity, gender, race, religion, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status - and (2) their environment - physical, cultural, political.
